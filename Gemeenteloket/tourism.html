Toerisme

SilverDome

SilverDome is a sports and recreation cent in Zoetermeer. The SilverDome has multifunctional ice rinks and can be used as an event location. There is an ice hockey track, a long track for skating and a curling track. Lessons are taught on all sports by specialized sports clubs. Recreational skating can be done on the ice rinks from October to March. From April to June, various events are organized such as concerts, dance, company fairs and marriages. From July to September there is summer ice for the sports clubs.

Watertoren

The Water Tower found in Zoetermeer has been designed by the architect C. Visser. Construction started in 1927 and construction was finished in 1928. A foundation called ‘Stichting Drinkwaterleiding De Tien Gemeenten’ requested the construction. The water tower is 48,65 meters tall and the water reservoir is 500 m³ in total. The square around the Water Tower has been decorated with multiple stones, containing the names of the members of the foundation. Those being Wassenaar, Stompwijk, Zoetermeer, Zegwaart, Benthuizen, Moercapelle, Zevenhuizen, Bleiswijk, Bergschenhoek, Berkel, Pynacker, Nootdorp and Veur. All coats of arms can be seen around the entrance, except the ones from Stompwijk and Veur. Next to that, the build year and the province’s coat of arms can be seen as well. The miniature town in The Hague called Madurodam contains a scaled model of the water tower.

Nelson Mandelabrug

The Nelson Mandela Bridge is a bridge for walkers and cyclists, designed by Johan Bak. The bridge was opened in 1992 and has a length of 180 meters. The bridge connects the Boerhaavelaan in the Driemanspolder district with the Plein van de Verenigde Naties in the Rokkeveen district and crosses the national highway called A12 and two railways, one for the local RandStadRail and the other for the national trains. The bridge passes through the building called the 'Poort van Zoetermeer'. The bridge was built to connect the new district Rokkeveen with the rest of Zoetermeer and the Floriade in 1992. The old bridge built in 1973 was too small for the expected number of visitors. The architect used the blue-yellow colours of Zoetermeer's coat of arms in the bridge’s design. The blue steel structure combined with yellow windows. The bike path and footpath are separated by an artpiece designed by Arnold Hamelberg. There are three escalators and a lift on both sides of the stairs. In addition to a bridge for walkers and cyclists, the bridge also functions as a station hall for RandstadRail station Driemanspolder and train station Zoetermeer of the NS (Nederlandse Spoorwegen).

De Grote Dobbe

The Grote Dobbe or Dobbeplas is the name of a dobbe in Zoetermeer. The inhabitants of Zoetermeer call it The Dobbe most of the time. The Dobbe has been used as a water supply when Zoetermeer was just a village. Nowadays the Dobbe lies directly next to the Stadshart, Zoetermeer’s city centre. On the north side of the Dobbe is a large parking lot which is used as a market place on market days and there are special events on a regular basis. Next to this parking space lies the town hall and the city centre, which has more than two hundred shops and catering establishments. There is also a catering facility directly next to the water. In the summer the Dobbe is a popular place to relax and enjoy the sunny weather while in the winter you can ice skate. Next to its recreational function, the Dobbe is a forage pond for urban poultry. On the northern side, a permanent work of art has been set up in the form of a spiral.

SnowWorld

SSnowWorld Zoetermeer is an indoor ski slope which opened in December 1996. SnowWorld Zoetermeer was the second indoor ski slope with real snow in the Netherlands after Skidôme in Rucphen. Currently there are two slopes with a length of 140 meters and one track with a length of 300 meters for the more advanced skiers and snowboarders. The first two have been there since the opening in 1996, the third track has been in existence since December 2003 and has been extended in 2017. In addition to the slopes, there are a few other facilities, including an après ski area, a fondue restaurant and a conference hall for meetings.
